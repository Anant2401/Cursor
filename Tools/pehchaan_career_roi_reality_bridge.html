How Much Will It Actually Cost?
And When Will It Pay Back?

Ballpark numbers you can take into family planning chats — always cross-check with your actual college fee sheet or a qualified CA when it matters.

How this calculator works
  • Step 1 (below): you pick one career track so we can load typical fees and timelines.
  • We add tuition, optional coaching, living-away costs (only where they apply), and a small buffer for surprises.
  • We compare that total to typical early-career salary bands — not a prediction of your exact offer.
  • Break-even is a simple “how long to earn the money back” at mid starting pay — round numbers to guide conversation, not a guarantee.
Step 1 — Choose a Career Path

Choose the path closest to your plan. After you pick one, the next steps open below.

Step 2 — Choose Education Path

Different college types mean very different total investments and outcomes. Pick the lane that honestly matches your situation — we will still cheer you on whichever you choose.

Step 3 — Coaching / prep (optional)

Skip this if you are not taking a paid coaching package. If you do, pick the style that is closest — online is usually the lowest total cost.

Step 4 — Where will you live during college years?

At home means no paid PG and no “living away” rent in the estimate. Tier 3 / Tier 2 / Metro mean you pay rent or PG while studying — we do not name specific cities here.

Step 5 — Miscellaneous & Buffer Adjustment

Books, medical, travel, internet, phone, unexpected costs. Add a buffer — real costs always exceed estimates.
