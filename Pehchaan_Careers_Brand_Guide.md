## 1. BRAND IDENTITY

### Name
**Pehchaan Careers**
- Always written as: **Pehchaan Careers** (capital P, capital C)
- Never: pehchaan, PEHCHAAN, Pehchaan careers

### What Pehchaan Is
Not a coaching institute. Not a placement agency. Not a consultancy.
A **life guidance platform** — helping students and parents make honest, informed choices about their future, whatever that future looks like.

### What Makes Pehchaan Different
- Built by someone who lived the broken system, not a classroom academic
- Covers ALL paths — not just corporate
- Speaks honestly, not what students want to hear
- Parents and students guided together
- Community model: guided students return as partners for the next batch

## 5. BRAND VOICE

### Personality
- Honest elder sibling who has been there
- Practical, warm, never preachy
- Says "here's what I'd actually do" — not "here's what the textbook says"
- Never uses fear tactics or pressure
- Never makes false promises

### Tone by context
| Context | Tone |
|---|---|
| Student-facing | Conversational, direct, warm, like a trusted friend |
| Parent-facing | Reassuring, credible, respectful |
| School/principal | Professional but human, evidence-based |
| Social media | Honest, real, sometimes provocative but never preachy |
| WhatsApp | Friendly, concise, personal |

### Never say
- "Guaranteed placement"
- "100% success rate"
- "Best coaching in Chhattisgarh"
- Anything that sounds like a typical coaching institute

## 6. AUDIENCE

### Primary: Students
- Age: 16–22
- Location: Tier 3 cities in Chhattisgarh — Korba, Bilaspur, Raigarh, Champa, Akaltara, Naila
- Profile: Average students, middle-class families, not toppers
- Sitting on last bench, ambitious but afraid, overwhelmed by choices
- First in family to think about a private sector career

### Secondary: Parents
- Key decision-makers, especially for stream choice after Class 10
- Fearful of wrong decisions, influenced by neighbours and relatives
- Trust credibility and personal experience over promises
- Need reassurance more than information

### Tertiary: Schools
- B2B revenue stream
- Principals and teachers who want career guidance for students
- Value: professional, free from coaching institute baggage

## 8. THE 9 MARKET SKILLS

Three categories — Foundation, Communication, Mindset.

### Foundation (Teal)
1. **Communication** — Explain yourself clearly. In person, on paper, on a call.
2. **Digital Literacy** — Excel, email, Google tools, basic social — every job needs this.
3. **Data Thinking** — Look at numbers. Understand what they mean. Suggest what to do.

### Communication (Amber)
4. **English Comfort** — Not fluency. Willingness to speak up. That alone sets you apart.
5. **Self-Presentation** — How you show up in interviews, meetings, and online profiles.
6. **Teamwork** — Every path — corporate, arts, govt — involves working with people.

### Mindset (Purple)
7. **Problem Solving** — Don't just report the problem. Come with a possible solution.
8. **Reliability** — Do what you said. When you said. Rarer than any degree.
9. **Adaptability** — The market changes fast. Those who adjust quickly always win.

## 9. BUSINESS MODEL

### Revenue Streams
| Stream | Price | Customer |
|---|---|---|
| Student programme | ₹3,000–₹8,000 per batch | Students |
| Parent counselling | ₹500–₹1,500 one-time | Parents |
| School tie-up | ₹20,000–₹50,000 per school | Schools |
| Corporate placement fee | TBD | Hiring companies (Phase 3) |

### City Rollout Order
1. **Korba** — contact exists, start here
2. **Bilaspur** — 80km from Korba, larger city
3. **Raigarh** — industrial town, parent aspiration is high
4. **Champa, Akaltara, Naila** — after proof of model

### Three Phases
- **Phase 1 (Months 1–6):** Free pilot, 10–20 students, one city, collect proof
- **Phase 2 (Months 7–18):** Paid batches, all three revenue streams, build content + expert network
- **Phase 3 (Month 19+):** City franchises, online platform, corporate placement fees

## 11. FOUNDER STORY (for Slide 10 / About section)

Very average student in school. Got through 10th. Wanted Commerce — ended up in Maths somehow. Joined BSc Maths. Then saw a newspaper ad for a new engineering college. Left BSc after 4 months. Joined BE Computer Science. Completed engineering but could not make it to campus placements. Travelled to Delhi because friends were going. Started at HCL BPO. No plan. No roadmap. Just figuring it out — one step at a time, through every hurdle. 16 years in corporate India later — operations, finance, business services — doing well. And one thought that never left: what if someone had shown me the path earlier? That is why Pehchaan exists.
